Batting Style and Technique :

1. Balanced Stance:

Posture: Rohit has a slightly open stance, which allows him to cover the ball well, particularly when facing fast bowlers. His head is still, and his eyes are focused on the ball, giving him a good sense of timing.

2. Elegant Drive:

Rohit is particularly strong on the offside, with a classic technique for driving the ball. He often plays beautiful cover drives, using minimal foot movement but excellent timing and placement. His balance when playing drives is impeccable, and he ensures that his head and bat are aligned to the ball, which contributes to the purity of his strokes.

3. Shot Selection:

Rohit is known for his selective shot-making, and he often chooses to wait for the right ball to play. He doesn’t try to force shots but rather looks for opportunities to drive, cut, or pull when the ball is in his range.

Cut and Pull: Rohit’s cutting and pulling technique against short-pitched deliveries is highly effective. He is especially lethal on the backfoot, using the horizontal bat to play cut and pull shots with precision.

4. Timely Aggression:

While Rohit can be patient, he is also known for his ability to accelerate the innings at crucial moments. His batting gears up quickly when required, especially in the middle overs of limited-overs cricket.

Six-Hitting Ability: Rohit has a remarkable ability to hit sixes, particularly during the powerplays in limited-overs cricket. His effortless six-hitting is one of his trademarks, where he uses a relaxed bat swing and impeccable timing to clear the boundary.

5. Backfoot Play:

A significant feature of Rohit’s game is his backfoot play. His ability to play the ball late on the backfoot is key to his success, especially when dealing with bouncers or deliveries outside the off-stump. He is extremely comfortable cutting the ball behind square.

6. Footwork Against Spin:

Rohit’s footwork against spinners is very good, as he uses both forward and back movements, depending on the length of the ball. He often uses the sweep shot to counter spinners and has a solid defense against them.

7. Mental Toughness:

Rohit's technique is supported by strong mental resilience. He often takes his time to settle in, particularly in Test cricket, and then starts playing his shots. His temperament allows him to stay calm under pressure, a critical aspect of his technique.

8. Unique Shots and Innovation:

Pull Shot & Hook Shot: Rohit’s pull and hook shots are executed with great control, enabling him to punish the bowlers who bowl short.

Scoop and Ramp Shot: In limited-overs cricket, particularly in T20s, he is known to innovate with unconventional shots like the scoop and ramp, often played fine behind the keeper to add to his scoring options.

Awards and Achievements :

Rohit Sharma is one of India's most successful cricketers, particularly known for his achievements in limited-overs formats. Here are some of his key awards and achievements:

---->Most Double Centuries in One-Day Internationals (ODIs):

---->ICC Champions Trophy (2013):

Rohit Sharma was part of the Indian team that won the ICC Champions Trophy in 2013. He contributed significantly to India’s victory with his batting.

---->ICC World Cup (2007 - T20):

Although Rohit Sharma did not play much in the 2007 ICC T20 World Cup, he was a part of the Indian team that won the inaugural T20 World Cup in South Africa.

Rohit Sharma was the highest run-scorer of the 2019 ICC Cricket World Cup, with 648 runs, including 5 centuries – a record for most centuries in a single World Cup.
